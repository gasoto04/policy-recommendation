The Government's initial policy response was to intervene in the foreign exchange market to limit the appreciation, while sterilizing the domestic liquidity impact of the foreign exchange purchases. # 1. Adjustment  strategy  

Given the stronger-than-programmed macroeconomic gains in 1993/94 and the more favorable external terms of trade associated with the higher international coffee prices, the authorities believe that it will be possible to ain for stronger macroeconomic objectives for 1994-95 than those envisaged in the program. Specifically, the aims are to: (a) achieve solid economic growth, slightly higher than projected in the program; (b) maintain single-digit inflation, in line with the program; and (c) keep the external current account in surplus in 1994 and in balance in 1995, both of which are considerably stronger than programaed; gross international reserves will be kept at a relatively comfortable five months of imports.  

The key to the achievement of these objectives is: (a) to lower real interest rates without reigniting inflationary pressures; and (b) to naintain nominal exchange rate stability--without significant intervention-- and preserve Kenya's external competitiveness by lowering inflation to a level broadly in line with inflation abroad. To this end, a very large increase in national saving is envisaged for 1994/95 through a budget deficit reduction of more than 5 percent of GDP, a further significant improvement in the operating profit of key public enterprises, and recovery of private saving: the latter will be facilitated by higher coffee earnings. With higher saving, pressures on interest rates and foreign exchange inflows will ease considerably. The CBK's credit policy and open market operations could then be adjusted to achieve the above interest and exchange rate objectives. Should pressures for exchange rate appreciation persist, fiscal policy would be further tightened.  

The other main item on the policy agenda is to strengthen structural policies, in particular to conplete domestic deregulation with petroleum liberalization, and accelerate public enterprise reform. The authorities have taken a nunber of measures to catch up with the program objectives where progress had fallen short. Substantial improvement in the financial performance of key public enterprises is envisaged, as well as strengthening of operational efficiency through performance contracts. Privatization will also be accelerated, including sales of some very large enterprises.

# 1. Ceilings.to expenditure...comnitmenta

These ceilings will be cunulative within each financial year so that ministries can spend in subsequent periods what they have saved in the current one.  

As a way of ensuring that appropriate quarterly ceilings are set, a mechanism will be put in place where (i) legitimate requirements of all ministries are properly accounted for on the basis of submissions fron them, and (ii) domestic financing is in line with the capacity of the market to absorb governnent borrowing of such magnitude.
